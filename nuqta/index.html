On accuracy
Transliteration is approximate by nature. Roman letters cannot disambiguate Urdu homophones:
  • s maps to any of س، ص، ث · z to ز، ض، ظ، ذ · t to ت، ط · h to ہ، ح
  • Retroflex letters use capitals: T for ٹ, D for ڈ, R for ڑ
  • Aspirated consonants use h: kh, gh, sh, ch, bh, ph, th, dh
  • Long vowels: aa ا · ee/ii ی · oo/uu و · ai ے · au و
  • Urdu → Roman: on-screen keyboard matches the standard Urdu Phonetic layout. Physical English keys get remapped when Phonetic Typing is on.
A built-in dictionary of common words takes priority. Unknown words fall back to character-level phonetic rules. Results will not always match a native writer's preferred spelling.
